Introduction

Singtel/Optus has a legal and contractual obligation to safeguard certain information and telecommunications infrastructure. It is a requirement that all projects involving offshoring, outsourcing, or that potentially expose consumer, business or government customer information to external entities, undergo review by relevant stakeholders.

This form assists you in gathering necessary information and speeding up the engagement and assessment process. Please complete the form as fully as possible and return it Information Security.

If the change impacts privacy, regulatory, or government and defence contracts, and requires review by the Offshore Working Group (OWG), Information Security will advise you, but a project representative must follow up with OWG to expedite approval.

Objectives

This form examines key areas of the proposed solution or change with the aim of identifying information security risks. The risk analysis verifies whether appropriate safeguards are in place, and that the design complies with relevant regulatory and Information Security policies. It also minimizes the risk of project schedule delays and cost overruns when security requirements must be retrofitted into systems and / or contractual agreements late in the process.

The Engagement and Risk Assessment Process

  • Complete the Information Security Engagement and Risk Assessment Form - The Information Security Engagement and Risk Assessment Form captures the data types, physical locations, the subjects requiring access and other key information. As the project scope may change over time, the form also provides a record of the information provided at the time the change/project was initiated.

  • Meet with Information Security - On receiving the completed form, a meeting will be arranged with Information Security to clarify and collect further information about the change/project.

  • Provide Design Documentation - This includes, but is not limited to application architecture, network topology diagrams, file schema and any other relevant system documentation.

  • Get OWG approval, if required - Where you have been informed that OWG approval is required, you should get this before going any further.

  • Arrange Security Testing - Performed prior to release into production, in an environment that accurately represents production, and not on a development platform. The test involves techniques to circumvent the application's security defences to discover vulnerabilities. Certified security specialists must perform the test. When completed, copies of all test results must be provided to Information Security. The project managers should allow additional time to remediate the issues discovered during the testing. The re test needs to be organised (if necessary) to confirm that the issues are successfully remediated

  • All deviations need to be approved by ISDC - If any Group IT Security policy deviation or risks are identified and can't be remediated before go live, the project needs to receive the sign off from ISDC committee. The detailed process is described here: https://espressoau.singtelgroup.net/depts/infosec/InfoSec%20Policies%20Standards%20%20Guidelines/Infosec%20Security%20Deviation%20Request.pptx

  • Get Approval - Sign-off is dependent on all applicable items in section 3 being met successfully.
